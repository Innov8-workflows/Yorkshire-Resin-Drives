How much does a resin driveway cost in Yorkshire?

A clear 2026 guide to resin driveway costs in Yorkshire — what drives the price, how it compares to block paving and tarmac, and how to get an accurate quote.

Get a free quote Call 07984 469764

Costs · 6 min read

Resin-bound driveways are one of the most popular surfacing choices in Yorkshire — but how much should you actually budget? Here’s an honest breakdown of what affects the price and how to get a quote you can rely on.

The short answer

As a rough guide for a fully installed, resin-bound driveway laid on a new sub-base in Yorkshire, you should budget in these bands:

  • Small drive (up to ~25m²) — typically the higher rate per m², as fixed costs are spread over a smaller area
  • Average drive (~25–60m²) — the most common size, where resin is very competitive
  • Large drive (60m²+) — usually the best value per square metre

Every driveway is different, so the only number worth acting on is a written quote after a site visit. The figures below explain what moves that number up or down.

What affects the cost?

Size of the area

The bigger the area, the lower the cost per square metre, because mobilisation, edgings and preparation are shared across more surface.

Condition of the existing surface

Laying onto a sound, free-draining base costs less than a full excavation. Most long-lasting drives need a proper dig-out and new sub-base — and that’s what our 15-year guarantee covers.

Aggregate choice

Natural stone blends sit at different price points. We’ll show you samples so you can balance the look you want against budget — more in our resin colours guide.

Edgings, drainage and access

New edgings and borders, channel drainage, awkward access or extensive groundworks all add to the cost — but they’re what make a drive last.
