Common resin driveway problems — and how to avoid them

Cracking, fading, loose stone and puddling — the common resin driveway problems, what causes them, and how the right installer avoids them.

Get a free quote Call 07984 469764

Technical · 5 min read

Done badly, any surface can fail. Almost every resin problem traces back to poor preparation or cheap materials — both avoidable.

Cracking

Cause: an inadequate or moving sub-base. Avoid it: proper excavation, the right depth of compacted base and movement joints where needed — our groundwork is built for it.

Fading or yellowing

Cause: cheap, non-UV resin. Avoid it: insist on UV-stable resin (we only use premium, UV-stable systems).

Loose stone

Cause: wrong resin-to-aggregate ratio or laying in poor conditions. Avoid it: an experienced installer working in the right weather window.

Puddling

Cause: a non-permeable base or poor falls. Avoid it: a permeable build and correct levels and drainage.

The common thread

Choose your installer carefully — see how to choose a resin installer. The cheapest quote is rarely the cheapest driveway over its life.
